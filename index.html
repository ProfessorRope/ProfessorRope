A Polyvagal-Informed Lens

Polyvagal theory offers one useful language for thinking about surrender. I imagine it as a guide; a checklist; and bundle of options that amplify our rope session together.

How Can we Create a Container for Possibility Together?

In essence our goal will be to creatively stir the different elements of the nervous system. At first creating safety and connection through regular speech patters, smells, sounds, eye contact, and mutually agreed upon touch. We can call this phase, co-regulation.

A polyvagal-informed rope practice might include slower entrances, explicit consent language, grounding rituals, clear exit signals, co-regulating voice, softer lighting, warm aftercare, and time for the body to return.
